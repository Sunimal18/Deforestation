FOREST-AI User Manual

Welcome! This guide outlines how to use the conservation analytics tools, interpret outputs, and navigate features.

1

Dashboard Overview & Map Telemetry

The Dashboard acts as your central landing portal, offering aggregated metrics and a telemetry-rich Leaflet map overlay of the Wilpattu Sanctuary boundary.

  • A

    Layers Toggles (Sidebar)

    Toggle active map features by checking or unchecking layers. Turn on **Disturbance** to see past forest losses, **Risk Prediction** for future danger zones, or **Reforestation** for active plot areas.

  • B

    Time Filter Sliders

    Adjust the slider or drop-downs to filter disturbance plots dynamically. Only logs within your chosen window will stay visible on the map.

  • C

    Alert Notifications (Bell Icon)

    Located on the right side of the navigation bar. Click it to view the top 4 most recent forest disturbances with their severity tags. Clicking any item zooms you directly to that location on the map.

💡

System Tip: You can click on any database polygon drawn on the map to trigger a detailed popup summary including coordinates, road distance, and AI explanations.

2

Disturbance Detection & Verification

This module monitors forest cover losses and vegetation declines using index measurements extracted from Sentinel-2 satellite data.

  • A

    Disturbances Map Card

    Features a dedicated map showing verified canopy loss locations. Hovering over a polygon shows its **Area ID**, and clicking reveals the affected extent and detection date.

  • B

    NDVI Change Metric

    Represents the Normalized Difference Vegetation Index change. A negative score (e.g. -0.25) implies localized canopy loss or land clearance, indicating potential logging activity.

  • C

    Confidence Scoring

    Represents the verified neural network confidence level. Scores above 85% reflect highly verified canopy reductions that require immediate patrol deployments.

3

Risk Prediction & Vulnerability Modeling

The Risk Prediction module models the likelihood of future deforestation based on proximity factors and landscape features.

  • A

    Deforestation Threat Score

    Calculated using distance measurements to the nearest logging corridors: **Roads**, **Villages**, and **Waterways**. Closer proximity increases the priority and likelihood of illegal activity.

  • B

    Explainable AI (XAI)

    Provides clear, natural-language rationales detailing why a zone is flagged as high-risk. This translates raw AI weight matrices into actionable explanations for field rangers.

4

Reforestation Recommendations

Suggests localized target zones for replanting efforts, optimizing resource allocation by analyzing soil suitability and threat exposure.

  • A

    Reforestation Suitability

    Categorizes zones as **Suitable**, **Moderately Suitable**, or **Unsuitable** based on proximity to natural water bodies and canopy health history.

  • B

    Reforestation Priority

    A priority metric (High, Medium, Low) that identifies target plots where tree planting will have the highest ecological benefit, focusing heavily on degraded high-risk zones.

5

Monitoring Reports Generation

The Reporting module allows you to compile, search, and download aggregated summaries of Wilpattu's forest status.

  • A

    Custom Date Querying

    Select custom start and end dates to filter logs, updating the reports list table immediately.

  • B

    Generate Monthly Report

    Compiles a fully-formatted, A4-printable Academic Deforestation and Land Coverage report for the selected month, suitable for submission to forestry officials.

  • C

    Export Formats (PDF & CSV)

    Download tabular views directly in raw CSV (for database analyses) or save them as clean PDF documents.
